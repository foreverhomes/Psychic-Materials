coming into focus.

This is a new love, strange, seductive and unfolding.
I’m stranded on these/by these sounds.
A new world where the feeling of familiarity slips through my fingers.
A new language buzzes in our apartment.
Did we build this? Did we construct these syllables into meaning?

June 13th, 2018
I push my memories through a sieve.
I struggle through perforated conversations.
A rustling of paper and a moment without speaking.
I fear my writing is boring.
I’ve become distant and dishonest.
Stretching feelings across mundane addresses.
I remember my first childhood home.
The façade was painted black and white.
I sat on a rock wearing a helmet aware of the impending danger of social life.
I should just be more honest even though I was afraid of honesty as a child.
The fear had me hiding in a plywood box on a constructions site.
My mother found me sitting on a ragged sample of carpet and eating Danish butter cookies.
She grabbed my ear and dragged me home.
I didn’t feel ashamed or regretful.
My queerness was the worst thing I could do.
It was the deepest shame.
An infinite no.
